Scholarships to Study in Canada

Several prominent institutions offer notable funding options like the Canada-ASEAN or Canada-CARICOM scholarships. While applying, you will need to prepare a targeted set of documentation:

1. Proof of Identity

A valid international passport or a national identity document with current dates and clear photography.

2. Proof of Enrolment

An official confirmation note supplied directly by the institute verifying your full-time educational placement.

3. Letter of Intent

A comprehensive personal essay (in English or French) detailing your course rationale, institutional selection, and long-term career impacts.

4. Letter of Support

A formal letterhead document written by a professor or mentor outlining the academic value and benefits of the scholarship to your future.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long can I stay in Canada with a study permit?

Your study permit is generally valid for the full duration of your academic program, plus an additional 90 days. This buffer period is explicitly provided to allow you to prepare your departure or successfully arrange for programmatic visa extensions through immigration channels.

What are some of the best part-time jobs I can do as an international student?

Popular options include on-campus student services, retail associates, restaurant servers, baristas, customer support agents, and academic tutoring roles. These provide consistent side income while seamlessly matching academic schedules.

What are the working hours for international students in Canada?

Eligible international students can work off-campus up to 24 hours per week during standard ongoing academic semesters. During designated seasonal holiday breaks or scheduled semester vacations, you are allowed to work completely full-time without hourly limitations.

As an international student, can I apply for permanent residency?

Yes. Graduating from an approved Canadian designated learning institution opens powerful immigration pathways. You can apply through the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) under Express Entry or secure targeted nominations via specific Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP).

Will I get an internship opportunity in Canada?

Many degree and diploma programs incorporate professional Co-op or internship structures into their primary layout. Crucially, under updated rules, eligible post-secondary international students do not require a separate co-op work permit to fulfill mandatory integrated program placements.

My Canada student visa application was rejected. What could be the possible reasons?

Common factors include presenting insufficient proof of financial means, lacking clarity in your purpose of study or your choice of course, or failing to establish strong home-country ties that demonstrate you will leave Canada when your visa expires. Re-application is entirely viable if you fix these gaps with added data.

Can I acquire a scholarship to study in Canada while I apply for admission into a university?

Yes. Many top institutions evaluate applicants for merit-based entrance scholarships automatically at the point of processing your main university application. Other institutional options or government awards require targeted documentation submitted early.
